Why This Problem Statement?

Understanding the gap that F1 data fills in consumer ADAS training.

  • → Normal ADAS training relies on normal driving data plus light collision scenarios—meaning models learn from low-severity, common events that don't represent catastrophic failures.
  • → Real-world ADAS failures happen in edge cases: wet overtakes, sudden deceleration from highway speeds, bad-angle impacts, hydroplaning during emergency braking—rare events that training data barely covers.
  • → F1 gives us a ready-made library of edge cases recorded under controlled but extreme conditions—a unique dataset that bridges the gap between normal driving and worst-case crashes.
  • → This makes it an ideal hackathon problem: small scope, clear demonstration, measurable impact (milliseconds and meters matter), and a novel data transfer approach that's never been tried before.
